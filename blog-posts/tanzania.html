Tanzania

November, 2022

Arusha

Experiencing the Mto wa Mbu village with a guided cultural walk and traditional lunch with a local family. Our first wildlife safari drive in Lake Manyara.

Serengeti National Park

Vast, sandy plains packed with huge herds of wildebeests and zebras nervous to approach the hippo-filled rivers for fear of the crocodiles and being hunted by lionesses. Herds of elephants cooling down, drinking their fill and basking in the sun. The Ngorongoro Crater one of the highest concentrations of wildlife in Africa, drawing in thousands of flamingos during mating season.

One favourite memory is calling my safari crew to attention, pointing out an oncoming herd of elephants.

"Oh my gosh. There is a herd of elephants on the way."

"Oh yes, they look nice," is the offhanded reply I receive.

"No" I respond with empahisis, realising they aren't seeing the whole herd, "THERE IS A HERD OF ELEPHANTS ON THE WAY."

And so it was. We were (well the waterhole was anyway) descended upon by tens of giant, but gentle elephants, just wanting to quench their thirst, wash and sun themselves, in our proximity.

Zanzibar

Touring the markets of Stone Town, a local tour of the Spice Plantation and relaxing beach days, with some awesome kite surfing and snorkelling, in Jambiani.
