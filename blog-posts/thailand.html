Thailand

First stop Bankgok and then up north to Chiang Mai to see the mountain ranges.

Elephants

They are such beautiful creatures – my new favourite thing, added to the list of things that I haven’t stolen/bought

  1. An Elephant
  2. My uniform from seeing the Elephants
  3. Hedgehog
  4. Portable home for the hedgehog.

They were hilarious as they made it very obvious that we served one purpose … to deliver a portion of their daily 200-300kg of food. There would be at least three of us standing in front of one particular female (in a herd of 35 elephants there were only 2 males) and she went from left to middle to right to get a stick of sugar cane from each of us. Sometimes I would go in for a cuddle and her nose would just be prodding my bag for more. Sometimes I had two in my hand and gave her one, she always came back for the second before putting it in her mouth.

Each one has their own “human buddy” and they make a particular cute noise to that person ONLY, not anyone else.

My favourite part was mashing some bananas with pellets of nuts and balls of herbs which was their treat but also to lines the stomach. While the mashing part wasn’t entirely exhilarating, they were rolled into balls and popped directly onto their tongues which were gigantic and so soft.

So this is what they do, eat every two hours, take a crap not caring where they are or what they are doing and take a bath in between. The life.

Ziplining with Flight of the Gibbon

…With the gibbons or looking like a gibbon, not quite sure. But zooming through the treetops of this rainforest was great fun, there were many lines to be flown.

We flew, rappelled, doubled up and jumped – this design in particular was unexpected. They strapped us into a chest harness and then we just take off without the normal seat and hook to hang on to. When I was ready to go I remember asking – you mean I just jump? Yup! So off I went. It was my guide’s first day apparently, but he seemed to know how to run the drill so I was comfortable.
